Establishment Background

The Korea International Leaders Congress (KILC) was established to strengthen the ethnic identity of 7.5 million overseas Koreans and consolidate the capabilities of the Korean people scattered around the world.

Our Vision

  • Establishing Ethnic Identity: Strengthening ethnic education and historical-cultural education for overseas Koreans
  • Global Network: Operating the Global Korean Network (GKN) to connect Korean leaders worldwide
  • Mutual Prosperity: Supporting mutual development of domestic and overseas Koreans
  • Cultural Promotion: Promoting Korean culture and traditions (9 Haan 1 Tong) globally
  • Peace and Unification: Contributing to peace and unification of the Korean Peninsula

Core Objectives

Education

Ethnic and historical-cultural education for maintaining Korean identity

Network

Global Korean Network (GKN) connecting leaders worldwide

Support

Supporting settlement of returning compatriots and overseas expansion

Culture

Promoting 9 Haan (Korean cultural values) to the world

Target Members

KILC welcomes all Korean leaders dedicated to ethnic prosperity:

  • Current and former Korean Association presidents worldwide
  • Peaceful Unification Advisory Council (PUAC) members
  • World-OKTA (Overseas Korean Traders Association) members
  • KOWIN (Korean Women's International Network) members
  • Ethnic educators and cultural leaders
  • Business leaders and entrepreneurs
  • Professionals in various fields

Founding Principles

1

Ethnic Identity

Maintaining and strengthening Korean identity while respecting local citizenship

2

Global Cooperation

Building a platform for cooperation among Korean leaders worldwide

3

Mutual Prosperity

Promoting economic and cultural exchanges between Korea and overseas communities

4

Future Generation

Investing in education and support for the next generation of Korean leaders
