2025 Korea International Leaders Convention

World Korean Leaders Convention

November 2025 (Scheduled)

Venue TBA

Capacity: 200 people

Event Overview

The 2025 World Korean Leaders Convention is an international event where Korean leaders from around the world gather to discuss the future of the Korean people and explore ways to cooperate.

Approximately 200 leaders from various fields at home and abroad are expected to attend, including current and former Korean association presidents, members of the Peaceful Unification Advisory Council, World-OKTA members, KOWIN members, educators, and entrepreneurs.

Main Programs

  • Opening Ceremony and Welcoming Remarks
  • Chairman's Keynote Speech
  • Keynote Lectures
    • Theme 1: "Great Transformation of the 21st Century and Korea's Future Grand Strategy"
      Lecturer: Prof. Son Yeol (Yonsei University Graduate School of International Studies / Underwood International College)
    • Theme 2: "Improving Voting Methods for K-Overseas Elections"
      Lecturer: Dr. Park Sang-chul (Former Vice President of Kyonggi University)
  • Breakout Discussion Sessions
    • Overseas Korean Education and Identity
    • Support for Returning Compatriots
    • Youth Overseas Employment Support
    • Korean Wave Promotion and Cultural Exchange
  • World Korean Leaders Awards Ceremony
    • Grand Prize (MVP of the Year)
    • New Frontier Award
    • Diaspora Community Contribution Award
    • Motherland Contribution Award
  • Banquet and Cultural Performance
  • Networking Reception

Participants

  • Current and Former Korean Association Presidents
  • Peaceful Unification Advisory Council Members
  • World-OKTA Members
  • KOWIN Members
  • Ethnic Educators and Organization Leaders
  • Overseas Entrepreneurs and Business People
  • International Student Organization Representatives
  • Domestic Corporate and Institutional Representatives
  • KILC Members and All Interested Parties

Expected Outcomes

Through this convention, the network of Korean leaders scattered around the world will be further strengthened, and practical cooperation projects will be promoted. In particular, it is expected to serve as an opportunity to strengthen the ethnic identity of the next generation of overseas Koreans and build a foundation for cooperation between domestic and overseas Koreans.
